## What this is

Mythology wikis already exist. This isn't another one.

God-Museum is a stubborn project. It takes the *Classic of Mountains and Seas*, *Journey to the West*, the Greek, Norse, and Egyptian pantheons — systems that have nothing to do with each other — and forces them into one shared cosmos, then demands they not fight. Why does the same lightning principle manifest as Zeus in Greece and as Leigong in the East? Why do the Chinese and the Hebrews both remember a world-drowning flood? Take those questions seriously and the whole mythological landscape has to be rearranged.

This repo holds the rearranged version. It's a setting bible, a project charter, and the foundation of a digital culture IP. Game writers can lift it as a world, novelists can lift it as a backbone, a digital exhibition can lift it for the visuals. Star it before you lift it.

## Why not put everyone on the same supercontinent

The first design decision you can't dodge. The "one big Earth, separate regions" route is the cheap one and the boring one. Zeus shrinks into a Mediterranean tribal god, Pangu shrinks into a North-China-Plain tribal god, and the whole thing devolves into a pile of local deities speaking regional dialects.

God-Museum goes the other way: **multiple realms running in parallel, joined by Gates**.

The Nine Heavens, Olympus, the Yellow Springs, Hades, Niflheim — in this museum they're real, parallel layers, normally blind to one another, connected at a handful of points called Gates. That lets Set wander into the East as a foreign plague-god, lets Mjölnir crack open a rift into the underworld, lets the sun Kuafu chases each night plunge into a trench in Niflheim. "He came from the West." "She descended to the underworld." Those lines in mythology describe crossings between layers. Taking them as cross-layer movement, literally, holds up much better than flattening the gods onto one map.

The three laws below keep this design standing.

## The Three Laws

Three sentences cover it.

1. **The Primordial Source.** Every mythology begins with the same explosion. Pangu splitting heaven from earth and Chaos giving form to the Greek cosmos are the same instant of creation, witnessed and told separately by the East and by Hellas. A god, in this project, is a cosmic principle that has taken on a body inside one specific layer.
2. **Resonance of Domains.** Lightning, the sea, death, the harvest — each domain is one thing, manifested in different layers as Zeus, Poseidon, or Gonggong and Leigong. Within its own layer each is a true god. When two of them meet, they resonate. Eastern and Western thunder gods behave suspiciously alike because they share one principle.
3. **Epic Convergence.** The flood Nüwa patched the sky against, the flood Noah rode out, the flood Gilgamesh survived — the same flood. Similarities this large are treated as evidence, not coincidence. They're era-level events that spread through the Gates into every layer at once.

## The Four Halls

Content is arranged by hall, not by country. The point of a museum is to see how artifacts from different civilizations look side by side in the same room.

**Pantheon of the Cosmos.** Gods grouped by domain and alignment, not by nation.
- Creators: Pangu / Chaos / Ymir
- Powers of Nature: Sun gods (Apollo · Xihe · Ra), Thunder gods (Thor · Zeus · Leizhenzi)
- Lords of Death: Yanluo Wang / Hades / Anubis
- Each god gets one entry: titles, office, origin, which layer they live in, and what happens when they cross over.

**Bestiary.** Non-human creatures, treated as more than monsters. Extensions of a god's will leaking past its body, or a natural disaster given shape.
- Eastern spine: the *Classic of Mountains and Seas* — Qiongqi, Taotie, the nine-tailed fox
- Western spine: Greek and Norse — Medusa, Argus, Fenrir

**Relics of Power.** The artifacts that actually rewrote the course of the pantheons.
- Eastern treasures: the Ocean-Calming Pillar / Xuanyuan Sword / the Demon-Refining Pot / the Investiture Scroll
- Western relics: Mjölnir / Gungnir / the Fates' threads / Pandora's box
- Once enough of them are catalogued, open a "Cross-Layer Weapon Bench": what actually happens when the Ruyi Jingu Bang hits the Aegis? This question gets answered here, as part of the setting. Not a stunt.

**Chronicles of Eras.** Every mythological plot gets jammed onto a single timeline. This is the part that makes the universe stand up. Three eras in, the pantheon has already been rearranged:

```
Chaos & Origin → Order & War  → Great Cataclysm → Margin of Gods & Men
 Titans           Titanomachy      The Flood Era    Rise of demigods
                  Battle of Zhuolu                  Trojan War · Fengshen
```
